What's in a name

Where we come from shapes how we age

The RAÍCES Lab takes its name from the Spanish word for roots — a nod to the ancestry, culture, and community that ground every life as it grows older. The lab is rooted in my own heritage as a Latina of Honduran and Puerto Rican descent, and in the people and places that shaped me.

We study aging as something deeply situated: in identity, in culture, in family, and in the conditions that make later life more or less equitable. The name also carries the six threads of our work:

  • R Research — rigorous, community-grounded science
  • A Aging — health and well-being across the life course
  • I Identity — race, ethnicity, language, and belonging
  • C Culture — heritage, family, and community context
  • E Equity — fairness in health and later life
  • S Studies — training the next generation of scholars

Our focus

Minority aging, studied with care

As a population aging and health scientist, I lead work that centers the experiences of Latino and other minoritized older adults — populations too often overlooked in aging research. We ask how culture, identity, and structural inequities shape health and well-being in later life.

Latino Aging

How heritage, immigration, language, and family caregiving shape the health and well-being of older Latinos across diverse communities.

Minority Aging & Equity

How structural inequities accumulate across the life course to produce disparities in how different groups experience growing older.

Population Health

Population-level patterns in aging and health, using rigorous quantitative methods to surface what drives differences between groups.
